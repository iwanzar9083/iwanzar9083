Opinions about the album
Many highlight the musical evolution of Bad Bunny. Reviews praise the fusion of genres and lyrical depth, noting that each song invites personal reflection.
Comentarios de la crítica
En Metacritic
The album received an average score of 86, based on six reviews, indicating "universal acclaim"
David Crone, de AllMusic
"Un Verano is not only a seasonal work, but also a testimony to Benito’s singular way of composing: across genres, generations and even languages, he works to produce lasting milestones that trace universal joys, sorrows and passions."
Lucas Villa, de Consequence
"The B-side is the more adventurous half of the album, pushing Bad Bunny’s sound to new places with collaborations with alternative artists. With a Summer without you, Bad Bunny continues to give pop music the flavor, arrogance and Caribbean sounds it so badly needs."
Jennifer Mota de Pitchfork
He writes that the album is a "coherently packaged journey through the various synonymous sounds of the Caribbean region: reggaeton, reggae, bomba, Dominican dembow, Dominican mambo, and bachata, among others"
